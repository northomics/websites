]]> https://ningzhibin.github.io/2019/09/11/is-imetalab-free/feed/ 0 Experiment VS meta information https://ningzhibin.github.io/2019/09/08/experiment-vs-meta-information/ https://ningzhibin.github.io/2019/09/08/experiment-vs-meta-information/#respond Sun, 08 Sep 2019 18:36:14 +0000 https://ningzhibin.github.io/?p=959 Read more]]> For example, we have a small project with two groups, 3 replicates each. We would like to set up 6 experiments, which will give us information about reproducibility. By doing so, you will be able to get 6 columns of quantification information. This is the Experiment information. We also need another grouping information, to tell the first 3 raw files/samples are from the control group, while the rest 3 files/samples are from the treatment group, to do the statistic analysis, e.g. looking for differentially expressed features among the two groups.

Meta info is not directly written/integrated into the result file, instead, it will be written into a separate .txt file, which will facilitate the iMetaReport.

The meta is optional, it does not affect anything during the automatic data analysis. You can add up to 5 groups of meta. Only the first groups of meta will be used for MetaReport (If there is meta info).
